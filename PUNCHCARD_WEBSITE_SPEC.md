# PunchCard Website Specification

## Marketing Copy, UX Structure, and Wireframe

Version: 1.0
Prepared for: PunchCard Marketing Website
Purpose: Provide a clear, conversion-focused website structure for prospective vendors and customers.

---

# 1. Website Objectives

The PunchCard website has three primary goals:

1. **Explain the product clearly**
   Help business owners understand what PunchCard is and how it works.

2. **Convert visitors into leads**
   Encourage vendors to contact PunchCard to start a loyalty program.

3. **Provide credibility**
   Demonstrate that PunchCard is a professional, secure, and reliable system.

The site should be **simple, fast, mobile-friendly, and designed as a single-page scrolling experience.**

---

* Coffee shops

* Benefit-focused

---

# 4. High Conversion UX Principles

The design must follow these principles:

### 1. Immediate Clarity

Visitors must understand the product within **5 seconds**.

Headline must clearly state the value.

### 2. Minimal Navigation

Single-page layout with anchor scrolling.

Navigation links:

* How It Works
* Benefits
* Businesses
* Contact

### 3. Clear Calls to Action

Multiple CTAs throughout the page:

Primary CTA:

**Get PunchCard for Your Business**

Secondary CTA:

**Contact PunchCard**

Email:

[info@punchcard.co.za](mailto:info@punchcard.co.za)

### 4. Visual Storytelling

Use:

* Phone mockups
* QR scanning illustrations
* Simple diagrams
* Loyalty card graphics

### 5. Mobile First

Most users will view this site on mobile.

Key mobile considerations:

* Large text
* Large CTA buttons
* Vertical layouts
* Minimal clutter

# 6. Visual Design Direction

Suggested brand tone:

Modern
Simple
Friendly
Local business focused

Suggested color palette (implemented, brighter for contrast on lighter backgrounds):

Primary: Red (#fca5a5, #ef4444) – headlines, CTAs, problem/benefit accents  
Secondary: Yellow (#fef08a, #facc15) – highlights, step 2, badges, footer tagline  
Accent: Blue (#93c5fd, #3b82f6) – step 3, secondary buttons, contact links, security/customer-experience sections  

Neutral: Black / charcoal (#0a0a0f) with white/off-white text (#fff, #e2e8f0).  
Hero and section backgrounds use a lighter overlay so the red/yellow/blue circle imagery shows through more; text uses subtle shadows so it stays readable.

Assets in use:

- Hero: Punch-card-logo-01.avif (full logo), Punchcard-pic-05.jpg (phone/loyalty).
- Nav/Footer: Punch-card-logo-sm-01.avif.
- Backgrounds: Cream (#fffef5, off-white with a hint of yellow). One very subtle pale circle per section (light red, light yellow, or light blue) as the only background accent; no gradients, no solid blocks.
- “See PunchCard in action”: Section has a short lead-in (PunchCard fits at the counter, café, till; convenience at your fingertips; customers have their card on their phone and loyalty gets rewarded; no extra hardware, hassle, or overheads) and a closing line ("Your brand, your space—PunchCard fits where you work."). Punchcard-pic-01.jpg through Punchcard-pic-05.jpg in a strip below the text.
- Favicon: favicon-icon.avif (in index.html).

Backgrounds: Cream (#fffef5) everywhere. One very subtle pale circle per section only. No gradients anywhere: headlines and CTAs use solid colours. One bold colour per section for headers (red #b91c1c, amber #b45309, blue #1d4ed8). Body text (paragraphs, lists): dark grey #374151 only, for strong contrast on cream. Section label bubbles (e.g. "The Problem", "The Solution"): light tinted pill background with bold accent-colour text (same red/amber/blue) so labels read clearly. Primary CTA: solid red (#dc2626). Nav and footer: cream with dark text.

Logo sizing (responsive): Nav logo 84px (desktop) / 44px (mobile). Hero main logo max-width 420px (desktop) / 260px (mobile). Footer logo 56px (desktop) / 44px (mobile). All via CSS classes so they stay crisp and bold on large screens.

Mobile rendering (≤768px): Layout switches to a single vertical column only; no two-column or multi-column rows. Typography is reduced (hero headline ~1.6rem, section titles ~1.35rem, body ~0.9rem). Section padding is tighter; hero CTAs stack full-width. Picture strip and benefit/step grids are one column. Nav “Vendor Login” and “Contact Us” stay in one row (smaller padding and font) on mobile. Getting Started step blocks each include a small thumbnail image (Punchcard-pic-01, 02, 03). Desktop layout and styling are unchanged above the breakpoint. Optional refinements: use a 640px breakpoint for earlier single-column, or allow two columns on small tablets (e.g. 640px–768px) for specific sections; adjust type scale per breakpoint if needed.
